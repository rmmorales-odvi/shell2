Cybersecurity Implementation Master Plan

A practical guide to implementing NIST CSF 2.0 across your organization

The 6 Core NIST Functions

GOVERNPhase 1
What it is: Establishing strategy, policies, oversight, and managing risk.

Why it matters: Sets the foundation, aligns security directly with business goals, and ensures leadership buy-in.
IDENTIFYPhase 1
What it is: Inventorying hardware, software, data, and mapping threats.

Why it matters: You cannot build defenses around things you do not know exist.
PROTECTPhase 1
What it is: Implementing safeguards like MFA, Antivirus, firewalls, and training.

Why it matters: Builds the defensive walls necessary to prevent or limit the impact of an attack.
DETECTPhase 2
What it is: Continuous system monitoring and hunting for anomalies.

Why it matters: Ensures you spot intruders rapidly before they cause catastrophic damage.
RESPONDPhase 2
What it is: Incident response planning, immediate containment, and mitigation.

Why it matters: Prevents panic, provides a clear playbook, and stops active breaches from spreading.
RECOVERPhase 2
What it is: Executing backups, restoration operations, and team communication.

Why it matters: Ensures business continuity so you can bounce back quickly after a disaster.

The Workshop: 4 Steps to Implementation

Step 1: Categories

Step 3: Policies

Drag essential policies onto a 12-month rollout calendar. Schedule minimums first.

Create initiatives for each category to track implementation progress.
